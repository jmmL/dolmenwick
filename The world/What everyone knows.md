This page summarises some of the information available in [Welcome to Dolmenwood](https://www.drivethrurpg.com/en/product/208099/welcome-to-dolmenwood).
# Folk
![[Dolmenwood folk.jpg]]
- **Humans** dominate the settled reaches of the wood, but they are not the only folk to call the place home. Mortals.
- **Breggles** (goat-folk) have ruled the High Wold region of south-western. Dolmenwood since ancient times. They divde themselves into two castes: the aristocratic longhorns and the commoner shorthorns. Mortals.
- **Mosslings** make their home in the dank, fungal-infested eastern lowlands. A shy folk, they are part mortal flesh and part plant or fungs. Mortals.
- A number of Fairy people venture into Dolmenwood and mingle with mortal folk: **elves** (fey), otherworldly and beautiful, the little cat-folk known as **grimalkins** (fey), and bat-faced **woodgrues** (demi-fey) are the most commonly encountered.
- If one ventures off the beaten track, innumerable creatures await discovery — many are sentient and have their own ways and customs. Goblin tricksters peddle fairy fruits and other questionable wares; scrabies emerge from their network of tunnels, eager to explore; even a bird or animal may surprise travellers by greeting them from the wayside, spinning a riddle, or cursing their trespass.
	- *You may be able to unlock new kindreds if you venture far and wide*

# Factions and powers
## The Duchy of Brackenwold
Based in the supposedly impregnable Castle Brackenwold on the south-eastern verge of the forest, the Dukes trace their line back to the earliest human settlers. All mortal folks pay fealty.
## The Pluritine Church
The monotheistic Church dominates throughout the Wood (and beyond). It holds much political clout, but is of late diminished: many shrines and chapels that once saw regular worship have fallen into ruin and been reclaimed by the forest
## The Cold Prince
Where mortals now reign, great fairy nobles once held court. The entirety of Dolmenwood was once the domain of a cruel elf lord known as the Cold Prince, who ruled from the palace of Hoarblight Keep overlooking the great central Lake Longmere. Once upon a time, he had plunged the wood into an eternal winter, but a great war prevailed between mortals and fairies, and eventually the Prince was banished to his domain. He has left his mark in folklore, and of those fearful of a return.
## The Drune
A cabal of black-cloaked sorcerers. They lay claim to the magical energies the wood provides, and guard such secrets with their lives. In the eyes of the Church, they are heathens to be eradicated, for it is said they deal in devil-worship and human sacrifice. Kidnap by the Drune is amongst the greatest fears by the simple folk of Dolmenwood, who prefer to call them “the Hooded Men” rather than speak them into existence.
## The Witches
Mortal women who worship ancient, forbidden, and forgotten deities in the depths of the Dolmenwood. Their aims are inscrutable to outsiders, but are portrayed as depraved wielders of hex and illusion, as well as ministers of potent cures and charms.
## Lady of the Mirror
A reclusive figure said to be the most powerful magician in the region. She dwells in the towering Chateau Mauvesse, overlooking the black waters of the Dark Mirror. *Further information is unknown.*
## The Nag-Lord
 A wicked, trickster figure of local folklore, said to keep unholy court in the twisted northern woods, where his armies of corrupted breggles and other creatures of Chaos lair. The Nag-Lord is known in folktales as the “King of the Wood,” and is portrayed as being part man, part unicorn, with nine legs (nine being regarded as an especially accursed number).

# Locations 
Roughly ordered from nearest to Lankshorn to furthest.

## The map
![[Dolmenwood blank hex map.png]]
## Lankshorn - 0710
The market town of Lankshorn, ruled by the breggle aristocracy, lies within a bowshot of the forest’s border. In Lankshorn, the traditions of the civilised south meld with the quaint, superstitious ways of Dolmenwood.
## The High Wold
A barony encompassing the south-western corner of Dolmenwood (including Lankshorn and High-Hankle), ruled in tandem by breggle and human noble houses. The barony has become wild of late under the lax rule of Baron Hogwarsh: highwaymen hunt the roads and charlatans peddle questionable wares.
## High-Hankle - 0512
The second largest town in Dolmenwood (after Castle Brackenwold), High-Hankle is the ancient capital of the High Wold region, now reputed for revelry, carousing, and debauchery of every flavour. At its centre stands the imposng Castle Perigonne, home of the Baron Hogwarsh.
## Dreg and Shantywood Isle - 1110
A port and fishing village beside the River Hameth. Dreg has a seedy reputation as a haunt for thieves, gamblers, and rascals of all stripes, only worsened by its proximity to Shantywood Isle, a notorious port of ill-repute. Dreg is also famed for its fine bog-swine sausages.
## Hag’s Addle
A shunned region of mazy swampland around the banks of the River Hameth, haunted by the sinister Hag. It is said that she glimpses into the past and future and resurrects the dead, but the price she demands in return is perilous.
## Prigwort - 1106
The market town of Prigwort is the largest settlement beneath the forest’s eaves. Famed for its breweries and distilleries, Prigwort is ruled by a council known as the Brewmasters. Connoisseurs across the land covet the fine spirits of Prigwort.
## Further away locations
- Castle Brackenwold - south-eastern seat of the Duchy, and the locus of the Church’s power
- Lake Longmere - a central lake, twelve miles wide at its broadest point. No ships ply the waters. A few keeps line its shores.
- Mulchgrove - central/eastern fungal forest
- Nagwood - northern, none of sound mind venture here
# Services
Occasionally it may be necessary for an adventuring party to take on extra help.
- Vehicles and animals (such as horses and hounds) can be hired, bought, or otherwise acquired
- There is a wide variety of lodgings and taverns to suit every taste and purse, including unique pipeleaves, beers, spirits, wines, and teas
- Retainers can be hired to help out adventurers in dark and dreary places. They will need a share of the spoils, however, and someone to follow.
- Other services can be procured in the right place for the right price:
	- Alchemists
	- Animal Trainers
	- Builders
	- Guides
	- Mercenaries
	- Pack Handlers
	- Rowers
	- Sages
	- Sailors
	- Spell-casters
# The calendar
The year in Dolmenwood is broken up into 12 months of approximately 28 days each, comprising of the four temperate seasons. There are a number of oddities, such as extra days, and occasionally unpredictable seasons. The full calendar is available [here](https://www.drivethrurpg.com/en/product/211097).

We start our game on 29th Iggwyld 1089, in very late spring.

The next region-wide feast day is the *Feast of St Clewyd* on 25th of Reedwryme in mid Autumn (the patron saint of Dolmenwood, in whose honour unicorn-effigies are burned atop bonfires and pheasants and partridges baked into spiced pies).

However there is a smaller festival, Longshank’s Day / Feast of St Waylaine, tomorrow, which is celebrated in Lankshorn and elsewhere.
