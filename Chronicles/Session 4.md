# Chronicler’s notes
Chronicler: Ilick

> *…five vessels in the dwelling of vessel known as Merridwyn Scymes. Recovering from a ritual of division. Breggle vessel…name of Nannigrew…concerned about tome. Depicting experimentation, anatomical drawings of Breggles. Religious, like the human vessel. Merridwyn vessel mentioned how there are no Breggle saints. Implications… Four of the vessels travelling with Merridwyn. Will travel towards. Contact will be made shortly…*

*Hague Jerricorn sent us on a small errand to Lankshorn, so we proceeded as carefully as we could given we were travelling on our own. It was a fairly uneventful and sheltered journey, but we were not ungrateful, given how much of Draxus Jerricorn’s material wealth we were carrying. We did consider giving the ring he had specifically left to her as we departed, since Shadow-of-Sorrows-Lament was no longer there, but she had stated we could have anything, and it may prove useful in future dealings either with her or perhaps in securing audience with Mosslemyre Drouge the next time we are in Prigwort.*

*Having completed our business in Lankshorn, we decided to wander up towards an area known as Shagsend, whereupon we discovered a large and curious group travelling towards Lankshorn. There was a Grimalkin Thief named Tip, a fellow Mossling by the name of Smodri, a Breggle called Nannigrew, and a fellow Magician Woodgrue by the name of Bogfrink. They appeared to be accompanied by another fellow Magician woman by the name of Merridwyn, thus, with such overwhelming arcane representation, we felt it was best to accompany them to wherever they may have been going.*

*It was this mixture of those sensitive to the arcane that provoked a rather curious discovery. Bogfrink complained rather loudly about a sudden shift in temperature accompanied by a sound they believed only they could hear. And feel, we suppose. But, of course, we heard a similar noise, a moaning of the dead, accompanied with a balmy heat and then a sudden, biting cold. Merridwyn also observed the phenomenon and told us it was time for us all to learn about “ley lines”.*

*Apparently ley lines are some sort of arcane occurrence spanning the earth that only those of our persuasion can really perceive, but the Drune people were intensely interested and protective of them. The others revealed they had come across an Audrune earlier by the name of Zarlac, perhaps looking after the ley line we were in closest proximity too, which Merridwyn identified as the “Ring of Chell.” She also intimated that the Drune had a number of sites that were very much off limits to outsiders, defended both by physical force and by other more impassable means. She said the one curious thing about the Ring of Chell, as the name would imply, was that it wasn’t a straight line like the others.*

*After helping the others identify a ‘Sen-Tree’ they had discovered, we found ourselves passing yet another red haired traveller on the road near Redwraith Manor riding on horseback and accompanied by an armoured mastiff. Come to think of it, we have encountered many red headed females recently, between Hague and Merridwyn and Robyn. We were given to understand it is a recessive gene and less likely to occur. Perhaps there is some correlation with the pigmentation and this area of the Wald, or perhaps to the occupations that we would come in contact with. A curious thought we might research later, if the mood strikes us.*

*Smodri called out to her for news, and she mentioned that Lord Malbleat is up to his old tricks in Redwraith Manor, rather disparagingly. She introduced herself as Robyn Tollmen, and said she was actually escorting a company of Woodgrues. They appeared to be pursuing some form of legend of a lake monster within a crystal cavern called “Big Chook”, whom they hoped to pass and retrieve the treasure of, which included a mirror purportedly belonging to the Queen of Witches and allowed for passage into their world. Lady Harrowmoor, the Ruler of Prigwort, was also mentioned.*

*However, our attention was largely drawn by the activity the group of Woodgrue were participating in at that precise moment, especially as Bogfrink rushed to join them. They were all defecating on a pile of very unusual fungi, purple and pink in hue with a shape not unlike that of their own ears. In fact, they seemed to be named “Grue’s Ear”, and would produce an effect of pinkish tint to the vision as well as a keen sense of alertness. It is, however, possible to build up too much of a tolerance to them, as evidenced by one sullen Woodgrue commenting that he couldn’t have them anymore. There was perhaps some suggestion they helped originate or propagate their own species, though this may have been simple Woodgrue whimsy.*

*It was about this time that Tip was frantically trying to get our attention, gesturing to some sort of object they had uncovered. We observed a rather large and fascinating grandfather clock made of a material akin to rock, but with shimmering veins running through it. We imagine Draxus might have been very interested. This did not, however, sit well with the group of Woodgrue, who were incredibly agitated for some reason. We suppose they did not want the rain to get on it? It is difficult for us to tell these things, being accustomed to all kinds of weather and dampness and things that many others would deem ‘unpleasant’. They did explain, with some prompting from Smodri, that it was going to be a gift for “Big Chook” and with Smodri’s wood kenning we learned the maker was some sort of artisan named Karlos. We will have to keep an eye out for more of their work!*  
  
*The agitation of the Woodgrue became a bit clearer to us when we noticed Tip was being casually held at sword point by Robyn, apparently trying to help himself to their material belongings. Nannigrew apologised profusely for these ‘children’ which made us chuckle a bit at the irony, but we kept the observation to ourselves. Tip at least seems far more pleasant a sort of thief than Shadow-of-Sorrows-Lament, more playful than entirely cold and self-serving. We remarked as such, hoping to help his case, but it seems everyone was very much on the same page, so to speak, no lasting harm done as we proceeded on our way.*

*However, towards sundown, as we were approaching the gates of Lankshorn, a contingent of shorthorn guards emerged, a piper bellowing to make way, as two phalanxes of the red and black dressed soldiers emerged, carrying some cages held aloft by sticks. Clearly working for Lord Malbleat, they appeared to be transporting some sort of wrongdoer, a couple of teen Breggles which Tip in particular seemed to focus on. They almost looked guilty? It was hard to tell, we are not the best at reading emotions. Maybe they sympathised with fellow thieves? These Breggle had oddly bright red hands, which we remarked may be some sort of ironic and ingenious anti-theft device and how they were caught. The guards said they would be attending “the Lord’s pleasure” so it was likely they were being taken straight to Redwraith Manor.* 

*Nannigrew also seemed a little perturbed, likely due to the species connection, or perhaps some religious inclination, Bredhr no doubt would have been concerned about the ‘troubled youth’ as well were she still with us. Questioning Merridwyn, she revealed a bit more about Lord Malbleat, saying that his rule of the area was only a couple of decades old, and that it had been originally under the auspices of a Ramius, who was much more beloved, but who had lost much of their territory. Lord Malbleat also appeared to be taking a very keen recent interest in magic and the arcane, to which Merridwyn seemed very apprehensive about, knowing she would likely be summoned by him soon being a Magician so close to his residence.*   
  
*Given everything we’ve observed thus far, it does not seem to be particularly prudent to approach him without some form of easy egress or protection, or more power backing our own arcane abilities. Even Draxus trapped his own apprentice in a mirror for over thirty years, and he seemed like a relatively benign personality. He kept a giant spider which he undoubtedly had to feed in a rather specific manner, as well as many other chimerical creatures, though… Hmm. Perhaps arcane study always leads to some form of detachment from the world and the connections to others when you are not so enmeshed as we are. We might ask Bogfrink about it at a later date.*

*The others were keen to get Merridwyn to Harper’s Hall, where the Berkmaster and his grandson were residing. Apparently the latter was ailing from some sort of curse or affliction that only she could heal. He was actually rather concerned, as they had apparently ventured out on this quest ten days prior, but relieved. Merridwyn was too tired to attempt the ritual that night, so Berkmaster Baldricke offered to let us all stay in the Hall until the following morning. Most of us seemed keen to do so, however Tip went out insisting something about being “loaded” and Bogfrink wished to first observe some form of lodging the others apparently were promised as reward for helping the Berkmaster. We believe we heard something about it having been a “Spirit Hlerribuck”.*

> *…the vessel known as Tip went to an establishment known as the Hornstoat's Rest, attempting to procure the most lavish of lodgings and treatment given his material wealth. …given an attic suite. One of two, other always reserved. …that one belonging to vessel known as Farnham, a well known performer* (ed: and sage). *Each provided with expensive substance for imbibing. …caused visions of small bald and clawed creature coming out of book and swirling green and purple vortex outside to Tip. Tip is returning…* 

*We had just been getting comfortable in a corner of the room when Tip came back to the Hall, loudly and drunkenly proclaiming there were colours and swirls in the sky and that we should look at them. None of us seemed to be able to see anything, but Nannigrew upon trying some of the liquor that Tip had seemingly ingested quite a bit of also claimed to see similar visions, specifically above the Nuncle Stones nearby. Curious, we examined the liquor carefully, discovering there was indeed some form of magical property to it that allowed one to see the unseen. Smodri, Nannigrew and even Merridwyn were offered a portion of this drink, we carefully declined. Apparently there were many invisible creatures washing dishes in the Hall that Tip attempted to accost, creating a brief invisible scuffle, but otherwise no other incident. How very peculiar.*

*The following morning there was yet another summer storm. The weather punctuated the rather cacophonous noises coming from deeper within the Hall. Apparently Merridwyn was attempting her ritual cure for the Berkmaster’s grandson Billy, which involved quite a lot of screaming and bestial noises. These came from behind a door that appeared to have been completely nailed shut at one point, and several of the others listened and peered through the keyhole to see what was happening. The sight that greeted them was strange, there appeared to be a half Breggle, half human looking child along with a wildly snarling badger that Merridwyn was struggling to muzzle and contain, too caught up in chanting and performing the magics to hear any of our calls to assist. Fortunately Smodri had the forethought to use his bardic pipe playing to enchant the creature, calming and charming it into being his friend, allowing Merridwyn to subdue it and complete the ritual safely.*

*Questions about said ritual resulted in her disclosing that Billy had eaten a mandrake root and that had, somehow, caused a badger to manifest inside of him? Bizarre! It was around this time that the Berkmaster, who had been worriedly waiting with all of us, told us about Billy’s origin as a child of Lord Malbleat and his own daughter Bronwyn, whom he had not seen in some time, one of seven wives of the Lord. Apparently human wives were not in fashion at this time. We shall probably never understand such formal and restrictively defined practices.*

*Smodri released the badger after administering some wanderlust enriched pipeleaf to it, causing it to swim excitedly through the lake behind Harper’s Hall. Then we all made preparations to journey onwards, securing transport, animals and most importantly, several doses of that same liquor that Tip had been imbibing the night before, knowing it would likely come in useful as we scouted out the nearby Nuncle Stones.*

*Pulling off the Ditchway towards the area, we discovered the Nuncle stones to be a series of trilithons arranged in a circular shape. Both Bogfrink and ourselves noticed and intense amount of arcane energy and magic swirling above the site almost a hundred and fifty feet in the air, while Tip went to talk to some nearby Breggle teens about what they might’ve seen. The group apparently had heard of a rumour of a black unicorn around here and suspected it might be related. Using the drink, we discovered there was indeed a swirling vortex above the site. Throwing a rock into it did nothing, but we surmised the matter might have had to be living, so we offered some of our new growths to test this theory along with some thread we wound around to secure them. Smodri initially seemed keen to offer his rather curious mushroom he was very protective of, but then quickly changed his mind when we pointed out it might be dangerous…and sure enough the mushrooms we sent through seemed to not only get sucked in, but when pulled out were nearly frozen all the way through.*

*Nannigrew mentioned something about a Cold Prince, and we went foraging while the others were discussing this, discovering several mustard yellow toadstools with purple spots which we gathered up for both study and later consumption.*

*Continuing further along the road towards Dreg, we discovered an inn or perhaps a halfway house which spanned the width of the road called the Jaunty Horn, which would perhaps be useful to note if we ever found ourselves stranded or in a bind on any other journeys through this area.*

*Eventually, however, we came across a rather more perilous encounter as we heard screaming and shouting of both Human and Breggle sounding voices. Peering through the rain, we discovered a group of pilgrims scattered around an upturned cart, looking in multiple directions as the forest around seemed to move and shift, almost as if the trees were fully ambulatory themselves. They told us they were devout followers of St. Paunch and that they were attempting to rebuild and restore a shrine to said saint, mentioning using a bit of rotting old wood. Of course, both Smodri and ourselves knew instantly what a mistake it was to assume rotting wood had no value to anyone or to a forest, but what was curious was their mention of the fungi growing on it: black and conical and oozing. We did not recognise the varietal off the top of our head, but something about the way they blushed when talking about it and the fact that their saint was purportedly one of prudishness in particular made us guess as to what may have transpired.*

*It was during our pondering of this that several Treoweres emerged to enact justice upon the pilgrims for carelessly felling and stealing their wood. Smodri did his best to try and negotiate with them, but they seemed keen to enact at least a little bit of destruction upon the pilgrims, even as Nannigrew got them to throw themselves down and promise to replenish the forest. Still, the negotiation seemed to prevent the Treoweres from absolutely annihilating everyone and an uneasy peace settled. After the bloody scene, the pilgrims pleaded and asked about the others of their number…*

> *…who were being discovered by vessels Tip and Bogfrink. …as the Illick vessel suspects: pilgrims taken by potent aphrodisiacs…vessel Tip confused. …vessel Bogfrink also taken in, joining the pilgrims in vigorous procreative activity. …vessel Bogfrink did not partake of the aphrodisiacs…perhaps some other vulnerability in kin. …will study. …vessel Tip very shaken and confused by events, running back…*

*…when Tip came scrambling back, clinging to Nannigrew, looking rather distraught, saying what a horrible, horrible sight he had witnessed!*
