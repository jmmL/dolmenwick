# Chronicler’s notes
Chronicler: Samwise
## Reunion
- Smodri and Nannigrew are awoken on the early hours of watch-keeping by loud partying sounds at the top of the falls - Robyn and her party of Woodgrue are on a booze cruise with the other party members.
- After joining up, everyone rests until morning
## The Spider
- Mid-morning, the party departs, aiming to visit the Balm Fields
- Walked into a web-covered area whilst lost. The trees had been touched by people called Lilibeth, Gertwin, Dimothy, Spindel, and Rogbert, with feelings of ecstatic joy.
- Moggert climbed a tree to figure out our location (we had gone North East, not South West)
- We found human corpses puppetted into performing a play about St Clewyd (ed - I misspoke during the session, incorrectly saying "Ser Chyde" when it should be St Clewyd) slaying a unicorn, puppeteered by a humanish spider. Someone remembered rumours of a cursed pupeteer named 'Auntie Spindel' who was turned into a spider after she performed a play offending Prince Mallowheart of the Frost Elves.
- The party clapped for Auntie Spindel, telling her that her name "carries" through the forest, whilst Shade gave her flowers. 
- She told us about her performance for Prince Mallowheart in Lampwrack (referring to him as 'Prince-who-is-Seven') and how she put on a political parody of the Elven court. He wasn't invited but showed up to the performance anyway. She also commented that the blue flute our bard was using was fitting and "takes me back to Lampwrack". 
- She has since been condemned to perform in the wilderness, though her travels in Fae had taught her that there are 2 kinds of unicorn: blessed and cursed, and that Attacorns are neither.
- When asked, she said that her marionettes were from Bogwitt manor and "willing, more or less"
- The party suggested that she build a theatre and pathway. She agreed to create a pathway from the river to that spot by the next time we saw her in return for the party agreeing to put out the word and draw people to her stage - though considering the creepy nature of her troupe, there was some ambiguity over whether we were agreeing to act as "Marketing or HR."
## A pastoral paradise
- We found a field full of animals taking afternoon tea, much to the chagrin of their farmers
- After rolling a "fey bullshit check", we remembered that Princess Andromethia presides over Blossom Fields, a kind of Fey Elysium
- The Farmers said that the animals taking tea happens "every summer" and they don't know why. Samwise, commenting on the economic opportunities of this, accidentally persuaded them to Rationalise their Pastoral into a slaughterhouse model of cheap, intensive yields of high-quality meat. They said that by next year they'd have set this up
- The party found a gazebo with a dog-eared book inside. It was a visitor's book full of questions in various languages with responses in gold ink and in Sylvan all signed 'A'. The creator of the bench beside the book was Princess Andromethia.
- The party asked the book a tonne of questions (delighting the GM with the opportunity to quickly mass produce enigmatic poetry in regular metre)
- Someone remembered that Princess Andromethia has a half-sister named 'The Lady of Spring', and that Mallowheart is estranged from his half-brother, the Cold Prince.
	- *Something impenetrable about Woodgrue working for Zarlac to find 'Big Chook'*
- Those who kept watch that night saw an elven woman entering the tend at midnight to write in the book.
## The Book
- When the party awoke there were a number of answers in the book to the previous day's questions:
	- Q: "What is your purpose" - A: A poem about the joy of a summer day and companionship
	- Q: "What fungus could clone a person" - A: A poem saying to seek out a "numbling fungus" and a "Skeletal gardener" (the party suspects this means the trolls that live under waterfalls) 6 miles north of Pellonias in Dwelminster (aka Drune Country)
	- Q: "What can kill an Atacorn?" - A poem drawing up an analogue of trapping a rat, suggesting that Atacorns are cowardly and will disappear in a puff of smoke if we do not find a means to contain it. (This may have been intended as foreshadowing!)
	- Q: "What is your favourite secret - A: "A circuitous Route Thinwhistle did take / Deep through the Avernal Lake / Midnight in Winter / An alliance in darkness formed." - The party interpreted this as Thinwhistle took a route through the Avernal Lake to make an alliance with a Frost Elf
## The Attacorn
- The party fast travelled to Lankshorn and did some downtime.
- The party learned that the magic flute was a Cold Prince court artefact that, if played under a full moon, will play independently in honour of him. It isn't cursed, but could tell stories.
- The party went to lay a trap for the Attacorn. Our plans did not survive first encounter with the enemy, but then again neither did the Attacorn. 
- Shade nuked the bloody thing with a flash freeze rune. Moggert went down but the work of two clerics was able to force some religion into its heretical little carcass and revive them.
- The Mayor's Wife was cured and the party received a letter of recommendation to Prigwort. 
- The party realised that the Attacorn was using the Hag as a deliveroo system for children. The party decided to avoid leylines as such as possible to avoid a reprisal attack for destroying her loyal customer.
## Journey onward:
- Samwise organised a funeral for the baby bones rescued from the Hag's domain, plus the bones in the Attacorn's den. Nanigrew attempted to 'Gaze' him into talking about the firebrands to which Samwise responded with a firm lecture on brainwashing, comparing her to the hypnotic goats in the Hag's lair.
- The party travelled up to a mill and had a peculiar interaction with a wise woman inside who tried to buy a crystal off the party. When Moggert tried to beguile her (brainwashing having worked so well 3 minutes ago), she replied that "I thought it's the crows that want shiny things" in Mewl. The party swiftly left and retreated back onto the boat to Prigwort.
