# Chronicler’s notes
Chronicler: Tip
## The festival
- The party met up in the early afternoon and headed to the festival. One of the new arrivals, Penny, took a keen interest in monitoring young Moggle. Traders in town were brining in supplies not just for Longshank’s Day, but also for the newly-announced festival of Hlerribuck, to be held towards the end of summer.
- Everyone tried their hand at something: Moggle was given a fortune that they will one day be a great catnip brewer; Bogfrink was bowled-over by Bringle-Brangle the goblin merchant, and purchased plenty of healing suppliers; Tip later returned to steal a valuable-looking amethyst; Nannigrew stone-cold knocked out her competitor in the head-butting contest; Smodri was able to get a winning ticket at the fairy-fruit shy; and unfortunately Penny did not find success at shin-kicking.
	- Lots of potions and balms were purchased; see Inventory sheet
- The party’s successes allowed them four winning tickets for the tombola, each granting mysterious enchanted objects.
- Part way through, the Berkmaster introduced himself to thank them for the rescue of Arda Vague (see [[Dolmenwood/Chronicles/Session 1|Session 1]]). He confessed that he required some help investigating why a magician, Merridwyn Scymes, had been so slower at delivering the promised cure for an ailment of his grandson. He can offer a very low price for a lease of a townhouse in Lankshorn.
- The party decided not to reveal the presence of crookhorns, nor the escape of Thinwhistly, to the Berkmaster (or anyone else). The party also asked around about the nightworms, learning that there is a much larger broodmother, and that some travellers have been eaten.

## On the road
- The party set off the next morning, following the directions that Berkmaster Baldricke had given them. Their travels took them past the well-guarded Shadholme (tombs of the Malbleat family) and Redwraith Manor (Lord Malbleat’s seat of power), but they pressed onwards to Shagsend.
- Shagsend presented itself as an unusual hamlet that had grown rapidly in recent times. It is also dominated by a large black tower in the centre of the settlement, where Hoblewort (the ruler of Shagsend, appointed by Malbleat, lives)
- Asking around with guards and patrons in the Crab & Copper revealed that recent expansion was at the direction of Malbleat, who is worried about intruders from the north. The locals themselves spoke fearfully of the Hooded Men, although many had more pressing concerns; there had been a minor village-wide brawl coincident with strange pipe music, just two days ago. Hoblewort was unable to calm/control the brawlers. The party rested for the evening in the relatively poor conditions the inn afforded.
- Setting off out of town, the party struck out into the wilds for the first time. Penny kept them on the track, and voices up ahead were scouted by Tip, who overheard a large hooded man conversing with a burbling/chirruping person. The man was persuading the other to scout Lake Longmere for a “sleeping worm/wurm/wyrm”, and also desired to “find the piper”.
- Deciding that it was wiser to avoid him noticing the party, they took a wide berth and pressed on. However, clever use of Smodri’s wood kenning revealed that the man’s name was likely “Zarlac”. In case they needed to backtrack to the glade, Tip had the bright idea to piss repeatedly on trees, sending Moggle into fits of laughter.

## The magician
- The first sign they were getting close to the cottage was a scattering of strangled woodland animals. The party approached cautiously, eventually spotting the cottage in a slightly uncared-for state.
- Entry to the cottage revealed uneaten food and unclaimed gold, as well as a smashed laboratory. No sooner had they entered, they were ambushed by a mass of skin and sinew with a mop of red hair. Some big hits from the melee members of the party soon laid it low, however.
- The party then discovered what remained of Merridwyn Scymes: organs and viscera held together only with blood vessels and nerves, languishing in a bathtub. Suprisingly, she was still alive. In halted speech, she explained that her own incompletely-researched spell was the cause of her doom, and begged for help. Studying her notes, Bogfrink was able to come up with a plan to spend a week completing the research with her.
- After a week, Merridywn was able to finally cast the completed version of *Split Personality*, and restore her body.
- During this week, some of the party ventured back to the glade in which they overheard Zarlac, discovering simply a hollow within a tree stump filled with clear water.
