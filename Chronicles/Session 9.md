# Chronicler’s notes
Chronicler: Ilick

*We rejoined the others after getting separated following a particularly fascinating network of Hound Game mycelia, which normally you would only discover amidst sand and coastal environments! We rediscovered them just in time to see they were recruiting a fellow Mossling by the name of Lambop Mossbeard, who appeared to be a thief by trade. We looked forward to getting to know them, certain they would not end up like poor Orrick back in Prigwort…*

*It was the 25th of Chysting and we had arrived at the Woodcutter’s Encampment, a place Smodri in particular was very keen to go to, making a beeline straight for Marrowbold’s Smoke Shop, seeking quality pipe leaf and pipes with which to smoke it! He met with the long grey haired proprietor, Megwynne Marrowbold, who immediately was able to offload three different enchanted pipes and two potently affecting pipe leaf blends in addition to some of the more mundane fare.*

*Outside the shop, however, we were concerned with several different sites, one being a nearby dark marble obelisk with a white rope around it. We noticed some worn writing on the side facing towards the buildings, perhaps shielded a bit better from the elements. Shade-of-Winter’s-Betrayal noticed the language bore some resemblance to the Drunic language they had been learning. We used our Decipher spell to translate what we could, revealing that it seemed to be discussing the presence of a Water Wyrd by the name of Gheillough that was a source of great power to the Drune.*  
  
*The locals, however, seemed to perceive the stone very differently, calling it the “Kissing Stone” and engaging in a custom wherein you kiss below the rope for good fortune and protection from being taken by the ‘hooded men’ whom the rest of the party have encountered before and ironically have identified Drune themselves, though their supposed abductions remain unsubstantiated. Shade decided to engage in the custom, kissing the stone purposefully on the side obscured by shadow.*

*We then all proceeded to examine the large tree in the middle of the settlement, which appeared to be quite ancient to the point of the residents actually having created supports for some of the branches and having roped off the area around the trunk so as to prevent anyone from approaching too closely. Smodri used his wood kenning on one of the lower hanging branches by standing on Nannigrew’s shoulders, learning the last person who’d touched the tree was someone by the name of Johnny Oystrous Pollard. It was around this time too, fortuitously, that an inebriated human came stumbling out of the Stump and Pollard tavern, shouting “Oystrous men arise! Boggle men be draggled!” Before ringing the curious large brass bell outside to a chorus of cheering.*

*It was around this time that Gwendolyn Crumwaller asked if she should go with the other party members who were immediately on their way to Prigwort, only for Smodri to vehemently insist on her remaining with us, “to see how her story ends” which we can somewhat understand given how he and we were the ones who initially met her…though perhaps our predilections and obsessions lie more in fungi rather than stories. Gwendolyn seemed unfazed and decided to stay at the Merry Mendicant inn.*

*We entered the tavern meanwhile to enquire about the custom and the bell, the establishment appearing to have been made from a hollowed out tree trunk and the floor covered in sawdust. Nannigrew attempted some subtle inquiry by trying to order oysters, which flummoxed the proprietor slightly, before we simply asked about it. It turned out to be another means of warding off the Drune, though it possessed no detectable magic at all. The people cited similar superstitions and missing persons as the Drune’s doing, though one rumour in particular about heartless corpses being discovered near Candlestone caught the party’s attention. Odell Overbleat was also said to have gone missing heading up there, Ramius’ illegitimate son, which was obviously somewhat of a big deal.*

*Thusly we all proceeded up to the Candlestone Fortress to investigate, after a meal of blood porridge and a side of codswallop. We found the fortress amidst trees of hanging yellow moss, crumbling and ruined and perched on the side of cliff, as if it were about to topple over it. There appeared to be a gradual path approaching the fortress or a climbing opportunity up the sheer cliff face, which it was decided would be slightly more tactical, in case there was something watching for anyone approaching. At the base of the cliff there were a number of large animal droppings with some undigested remains and the evidence of previous climbers embedded into the cliff via pitons and the like. Lambop Mossbeard proceeded to climb up first to scout the route just as Shade-of-Winter’s-Betrayal proclaimed the droppings to belong to an Owlbear.*

*Said Owlbear resided in a cave halfway up the cliff and was not especially pleased at being disturbed, immediately going for Lambop and rending them in half before Shade-of-Winter’s-Betrayal was able to messily finish it off. We held a traditional Mossling ceremony for our comrade, committing them once again to the earth while the others searched the cave, turning up some assorted gems and treasures, before we tried to figure out how to continue our ascent. We managed to use our thread whistling to start to secure a rope above, only for a large amount of rocks to be dropped down on us from above. Shade-of-Winter’s-Betrayal was able to move through shadow to appear just below the fortress, seeing a number of giant rats curiously scrabbling at the edge of the cliff looking down at us. The disturbance caused us to fall and injure ourselves rather badly, but fortunately between Nannigrew’s healing and Smodri’s pipe music charming two of them, in addition to Shade-of-Winter’s-Betrayal securing the rope from the top, we were able to ascent.*

*And not a moment too soon, for there appeared to be a large and fearsome dark winged creature stalking the sky that Smodri identified as a Peryton, who’s cry could apparently turn one’s own shadow against oneself! We all quickly entered the fortress through the nearest door, ending up in a dark, dank dining room. It had moulded over, which we were obviously quite interested in, a brown and and while varietal, though the mould appeared to be completely inert and lifeless. Nannigrew noticed there was a particularly malevolent presence that seemed to draw back from her sacred aura, and Shade-of-Winter’s-Betrayal revealed them through her bronze chimes to be a number of ghosts of individuals of particular cruelty.* 

*We fled to an adjoining room whereupon we discovered a painting of a door on the wall right next to a portrait, the wooden frame of which told Smodri Odell Overbleat had been there last. The portrait also depicted a noble elven looking individual reflected seven times with a red, rune etched candle, that the others identified as “The Prince Who Is Seven”, someone they had heard tell of in their last adventure. The notable sconce next to the painted door and the portrait suggested lighting an appropriate candle in it would make the door useable, but we had no way of determining it.*  
  
*The next room had a large stone spiral staircase with several steps missing in certain areas and some collapsed rubble, under which we discovered the body of another adventurer who had some gold, but more importantly, the same red rune etched candle we’d seen in the portrait! As we dug through the rubble a bit more we heard the noise of someone coming down the spiral staircase and were very surprised to see our old acquaintance Hollowgowl, who had apparently found themself in Candlestone Fortress after getting lost searching for fungi, which we could obviously relate to!*

*Using the candle we were able to enter a large room through the painted door which appeared to be full of black grass that was attracted towards light. There were even shadowy butterflies. But the most curious thing was some sort of personal chandlery set up not only to construct said rune etched candles, but to study and perfect them. An examination of notes revealed the individual was attempting to unseal the “First King”, dating back to almost nine hundred years, and stating that “the forest leads nowhere, the First King is sealed still". They were doing so by studying candle magic, which is presumably what the workshop was for and how the painted magical doorways were developed. There was in fact a very large painted door in this room that had a pedestal for a large candle which appeared to have been used recently, though we could not activate it without a similar candle. They also apparently had a taste for blackcurrant whiskey what with the silver flask of it we discovered nearby.*

*Continuing to explore the rooms around the large central one, we came upon a pile of twelve corpses of other adventurers, all with their hearts torn out, though Odell Overbleat was still not among them. Perhaps he had gone through the large painted door? We then came upon a group of giant rats that we made short work of, even personally disposing of two of them with sheer physical force rather than magic.*

*We then came across a curious library that appeared to have been sealed, though a crumbling wall and Smodri’s keenness to pick a lock meant we were able to see the repository was looked after by an animated skeleton by the name of Cyrilum. He was a bit of a curmudgeon and did not want to be disturbed nor have the library used, citing that we ourselves seemed ‘more of a practical type’ than bookish academic, but a bit of charming and pipe leaf sharing enabled us to procure a small journal about the First King Lampwrack.*

*Upstairs we discovered a bathroom with a tub following of a dark frothing sludge which was actually cultivating a bed of Mawbarg’s Jam, a curative slime mould of which Hollowgowl and us were very keen to collect. We then spotted a nest of Peryton on an open balcony and hastily moved away to what appeared to be some sort of bedroom, wherein we discovered the red rune candles could reveal shadow cloaked objects, finding a chest, as well as an cupboard with notably seven golden robes, possibly tied to the Prince Who Is Seven.*

*Eventually it became clear we would have to inspect the Perytons’ nest and deal with the creatures, so we set up a funnel point and tried to group them up so we could put them to sleep and dispatch them easily. Thankfully, none of them seemed to be able to do their fabled turning our shadow against us ability, but they still fought pretty bloodily. While we managed to cause one to fall asleep and be dispatched instantly, and Shade-of-Winter’s-Betrayal and Hollowgowl tried to deal with the other two as quickly as possible, one still managed to get the better of Hollowgowl and tear out their heart. Despite finishing one off, the offending Peryton managed to escape barely clinging to life and we were left to collect the body of our friend and inspect the nest, where we found a large red rune etched candle, perfect for using in the room below…*
