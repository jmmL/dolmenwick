# Chronicler’s notes
Chronicler: Ilick

> *Smodri helped to decipher the map we recovered from Draxus Jerricorn’s basement dwelling, apparently detailing the location of his stash of spare spell books, which of course would be very much of interest to ourselves as well as many others we are sure. While some of our number returned to sleep, the rest of us began to search Holstra’s bedroom more thoroughly now she had been dealt with.*   
>   
> *Smodri discovered a shoebox under the bed with three spell scrolls, one in Old Woldish which appeared to be for the Control of Monstrosities. The other two were in languages unfamiliar to any of us, but a combined effort of Smodri’s deciphering skills and our own arcane deciphering magic let us know that they were spells for Control of Plants and Protection from Constructs. Clearly someone sought a lot of control within their life!*  
>   
> *Searching the surface of the writing desk, we discovered a number of papers, five of them being some sort of patterns for a milliner while another appeared to be a letter written in the same language as the “Protection from Constructs” scroll with a number of serpent motifs around it. Arcane deciphering later revealed it to be a letter to Holstra from her ‘sister in Limwdd’, Grwith. They were apparently engaged on some form of quest to locate the “Hoaghryn” and also appeared to be focused on acquiring details of girls born in Lankshorn under the Waxing Maiden moon. Perhaps some sort of arcane ritual? Maybe it was what brought about her undead state? It is difficult to say without further context and study.*  
>   
> *Nannigrew began to search through the rest of the room with her added height and size, discovering a fair number ceremonial burial herbs, but not discovering much within the eaves of the attic.*   
>   
> *It was, however, at this point, that we were joined by a new person, an elf fighter by the name of Dapple-of-Moon-on-Water, who had been woken up in the room below by the sound of our battle and frantic searching and justifiably wanted to know what was going on. After a hasty explanation of the situation, as well as a promise to share in the loot in exchange for her help, Dapple quickly agreed to search with us and make sure the attic was well and truly excavated.*  
>   
> *She slashed open the mattress to reveal that it was packed full of six thousand gold pieces, then opened the wardrobe and discovered some enchanted leather armour with liturgical writing embossed on it, a devotional to St. Nuncy, whom apparently had a shrine the others were seeking near Lankshorn before we met.*   
>   
> *We also discovered several curiously pertinent tomes within the drawers of the writing desk! First, a book entitled “The Utilisation of Bad Unicorns”, which appeared to detail uses for the parts of creatures like Atacorns and seemed to be written by someone opposed to them and wanting to hunt them at the very least.*   
>   
> *Smodri discovered a book which, upon opening had a curious magical blue pipe inlaid within a hollow in the pages, with his wood kenning detecting that it belonged to a “Weeps-Until-Morning” and had a chilling temperature effect to the music played from it.*   
>   
> *Nannigrew found a book entitled “The Thousand Saps of Orbswallow” which appeared to be a rather thorough recipe tome on brewing and making tinctures and the like with plants and fungi found within the wood. A bit of consultation revealed a likely treatment for our condition could perhaps be found with an infusion of Arrowhame.*  
>   
> *Dapple found a book that took a bit of magical deciphering…and even then it was in mirrored writing for some reason that escapes us, given that it appeared to be a number of rather bawdy tales about a St. John. Perhaps it requires even more levels of deciphering to reveal its true nature…or perhaps the author really did not wish for their potentially sacrilegious or naughty text to be seen by prying eyes?*
> 
> *Regardless, at this point we all decided to sleep for the night. Upon waking the next morning we had breakfast with a number of other patrons of the House of the Harridwyn, with the two Auckland sisters happily holding court amongst us all. We dimly wonder if perhaps they might have been aware of their relative in the attic, if they in fact were the ones who kept her there or if they were some form of coven of witches given their appearance and the rather unusual nature of the home.*
> 
> *Smodri decided to try and get some information about potential cures for our condition rather cleverly by making it into a story he performed and then was searching for an ending. They suggested a “Nymph Pool” that they knew of nearby within the woods, slightly off the beaten path to the south, which they would visit occasionally to revitalise their own bodies. They also assumed we were all here to visit the falls and the Balmfields below them, reached via ‘escalator’ as that is apparently what most visitors come to see. The suggestion arose they could perhaps monetise the location of the Nymph Pool and advertise its healing properties.*
> 
> *We sent the rest of the party back to Dreg with a significant amount of the treasure while we pressed on to find said pool. After pushing through a lot of brambles and blue thistles and wood kenning revealing no clear or obviously named path, we did eventually discover a small pool of pink and placid water in a clearing surrounded by twelve statues depicting nymphs in various poses of reverie and play. The water appeared to be fizzing slightly as well, effervescent, in fact.*  
>   
> *We offered to submerge ourselves first, finding the water rather soothing and pleasant, and after some time, it did seem to heal our affliction! The others who had been afflicted by Holstra also submerged and found themselves cleansed as well. Clearly the sisters had not been deceitful in their tale of this place. We considered the logistics of how the waters might work, whether it would be helpful to bring a sample of it back to the mayor’s wife in Dreg or if it would be more practical to arrange to have her transported here. We did not have any vials or anything on our person, or else we certainly would have taken samples for study in general, though it is possible one of the others had the forethought to do so and we did not notice, basking in our recovery.*  
>   
> *Dapple searched for herbs and the rest of us joined in the searching to find the edge of the wood, seeing a circle of stones clad in white ivy as well as a large boulder to the east, surrounded entirely by trees. Our combined knowledge of plants knew that unfortunately white ivy was unlikely to be Arrowhame, which is normally green, otherwise we might have foraged it as a more practical treatment to transport to Dreg.*  
>   
> *The group was curious about the overlarge boulder in the circle of trees, and was wary of the stone circle given we were fairly confident Crewythiant either came from or went through the portal at the Nuncle stones, so we approached, finding a curious set of many unmarked graves dug around the boulder, the earth worked up around it. Smodri detected a large amount of fear and terror from the wood of the trees, as well as mostly local High Wald names.*   
> *Wanting answers, Dapple set about opening one of them, finding some grave goods in the form of four hundred silver pieces, which she immediately dropped, silver being anathema to her fey nature, we notice. There was also a belt apparently made from fish skin. Almost all of us, however, felt something keenly at that point, a wind beginning to blow from the direction of the circle of stones, and we quickly decided not to push our luck, reinterring the goods and the grave and moving away quickly towards the falls…but not before Smodri looked back and saw an eerie antlered monster peering out from behind the circle of stones, which he identified as an “Antler Wraith”.*
> 
> *Night was falling, so we decided to make camp and forage for suitable food and supplies, Smodri locating some Prancing Mandrakes, which apparently have a very potent soporific effect! Nannigrew then cooked us a decent meal while Smodri played us a lovely tune to wile the evening away, and we retired to sleep while the others took turns watching.*  
>   
> *Smodri woke us in the night having noticed what appeared to be violet eyes in the darkness, as well as an orange glow. Cautiously inspecting and approaching, we saw what appeared to be two very large owls, as big as ourselves, transfixed by a glowing orange crystal, which one of the others pointed out they had seen before, recovered from the corpse of a Drune, though it appeared dark and inert.*  
>   
> *Smodri attempted to charm or soothe the transfixed owls with music, only to seemingly provoke them, causing them to attack with a truly deafening screech. Each of us scattered into the night, knowing the owls likely had the advantage of sight, also stoppering our ears to prevent any effects their continued screeching might have. This unfortunately did not seem to help Dapple, who stared into what we later discovered was the third eye of this Witch Owl creature, draining her of her common sense as well as a much deeper seated ambition of exploring the woods. It did not, however, dampen her fighting ability, in which she cleaved the Witch Owl swooping and clawing at her in one strike, bisecting it completely.*  
>   
> *Regrouping in the dark, Smodri found a hollow of a tree to hide himself in while luring the remaining Witch Owl with music. Though it managed to resist at first and get a few claws in, we were able to drop it to sleep with our Vapours of Dream, and it was shortly dealt with after. One even appeared to have some feathers made from silver, which Dapple of course could not touch. We decided to head back to the Nymph Pool in the morning to help her recover from what the Witch Owls did to her, though while it did heal her spirit it seemed it couldn’t restore that ambition it took from her.*  
>   
> *Deciding to follow the path to the Falls now, we moved aside as we heard and then saw a curious commotion, a group of six humans ringing loud bells and driving away a large black goat with three red eyes. When questioned, they revealed it had ambushed them at the falls and had attacked their seventh companion, Henry. Wanting to make sure if Henry was okay, Nannigrew had one of them lead us to him, only to discover he was very much dead. Then we were delivered some rather unpleasant news: apparently Lord Malbleat, in addition to all the other unsavoury activities we’ve already heard, has decided to levy an adventurer’s tax, enforcing it across all towns and settlements within his domain.*  
>   
> *We weren’t especially ill opposed towards him, after all, we pursue many questionable methods in our research and study ourselves, but this does seem to be a rather pointed thorn in our side. We will have to see how this affects us truly later in the journey.*  
>   
> *Regardless, finding Henry’s corpse led us to the Falls, where we discovered the toll house and the curious ‘elevator’ allowing sailing vessels to move up and down the falls entirely of their own accord, which was of course a magic we were keen to examine. As we took one of the ships down, the ferryman said it was due to someone by the name of “Mister Nulley” doing something to the falls that they apparently had not the slightest curiosity to question.*  
>   
> *Our examination of the magical water led to the discovery of a large cave behind the falls, which we all explored, though carefully, given the large piles of both old and recent bones and a very rancid smell. Thanks to a brilliant use of a silencing spell from Nannigrew, we were able to sneak in just far enough to behold a ten foot tall, fleshy creature that seemed to be tending to a moss garden cultivated on a number of skulls, which it would then appear to eat.*   
>   
> *Were it just Smodri and ourselves, perhaps we may have been able to converse with such a creature and not fear dismemberment and beheading given our lack of skeletons and affinity with mosses and the like. We certainly would be curious to consult with such a creature in a controlled environment if possible. However, common sense once again prevailed, and we decided to leave for the Balmfields instead, setting up camp to rest for the night…*
