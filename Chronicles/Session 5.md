# Chronicler’s notes
Chronicler: Ilick

*We tried to question what Tip had seen, only to be told that something horrible was happening to Bogfrink deeper into the woods. As it appeared several other Treoweres were still in the area and still rather hostile towards any intruders or perceived woodcutters, we sent the rescued pilgrims towards the Jaunty Horn with the promise that we would direct the remainder there once we found them.*

*As we arrived at the scene in the wood we discovered four humans, one breggle and Bogfrink in rather compromising positions amidst a bed of tall, conical black oozing fungi. Naturally there was some sort of potent aphrodisiac at work, but we didn’t have the luxury of doing a prolonged analysis and sample of the curious specimens given the Treoweres stalking the area. Smodri was able to charm a couple of the pilgrims with his pipe, Tip gathered up some swamp water to splash and snap Bogfrink out of it, much to Bogfrink’s apparent chagrin, and Nannigrew did the same to another.*   
  
*We thought perhaps some percussive maintenance and sound based therapy would help the remaining pilgrim using our cooking pots and our hammer. Unfortunately while this did help them, it also alerted the roaming Treoweres as to our location so we quickly got everyone back to the road and then sent them on their way before we could be attacked. Fortunately, we were able to recover some small samples of the fungi on the inside of our cookware for later examination, as it is likely the Treowere stamped down all the fruiting bodies when they arrived at our previous location. Regardless, we pressed onwards to the east.*

*We arrived at a crossroads with some curiously old wooden signs pointing in each direction, of note being the northern sign to Hag’s Addle, the southern sign to a place called the House of Harridwan and the path being indicated as “Harrids’ Path”, in addition to the roads to Dreg and Lankshorn being indicated. We had, of course, passed by here on our own way to Lankshorn, but didn’t have the luxury of stopping to see all these little sights and take in the port town proper that was Dreg, at the edge of the Breggle domain, apparently.*

*The Mermaid’s Arms Inn seemed to be the most reputable establishment in such a hardened area, with a mermaid themed statue and fountain erected nearby, being used as some form of wishing well given the coins gleaming within the water. Clearly times were not so desperate as to be stealing from said fountain, as when Smodri threw in a coin and Tip tried to recover it and others, considering such activity a ‘waste’, many locals immediately glared and called Tip a “Pool Dredger”. Inside the Mermaid’s Arms, we were given lodging and a place to store our cart and horses, though the fact gambling, lewdness and sea shanties were prohibited meant the bulk of the party would not be enjoying themselves here and would doubtless be searching for entertainment elsewhere.*  
  
*Nannigrew, however, seemed to be on a mission to cleanse Bogfrink from the earlier experience, which struck us as somewhat odd given how we’ve been observing the breggle friar, but of course, religion and spirituality are not our area of expertise and we have other priorities to attend to. Luring him with promises of ale at the Shodgerey's Shack or the Spawning Salmon first was particularly diabolical, this is clearly not her first time with the bait and switch manoeuvre . As we headed to the Church of St. Wick, the patron saint of ferry folk and amputees, we were quite pleased to discover the church was incredibly dilapidated and sinking into the water, giving rise to all sorts of mould and lichen and even algae that we simply had to examine. Thus our attention was somewhat diverted from the others.*  
  
*We did notice that they were interested in an old woman who apparently is the vicar of the church, a Reverend Mother Hadlewreath. She was imbibing ale and looking through a truly scratched up monocle, poring over pages of a pamphlet which, on closer inspection, we’re told contained hand written passages on “The Year My Life Went To Shit”. We suppose most religious text is derivative, in the end, this is simply cutting out the pretence? She suggested sending Bogfrink to the Isle of Shantywood, which did cause ourself and Smodri to exchange glances, though Bogfrink and Tip seemed innocently oblivious. Nannigrew appeared to believe it to be a spiritual retreat, though again, we have our doubts.*

*Tip clearly thought more immediate cleansing was needed, finding the font of holy water and immediately beginning to try and douse a disconsolate Bogfrink with it, not improving his mood. This did, however, seem to lure out another, much more vociferous religious figure, a booming middle aged man who joined in enthusiastically with a kind of bombastic fervour. We came to learn he was Brother Roger Hogbeard, on a mission to reform and save Dreg from the iniquity it had fallen into, though what was more curious was the fact several altar boys dressed in yellow immediately inquired of him whether Bogfrink needed to be “taken out to the woods” as if this were a common occurrence with the congregation. We doubt they meant to visit the shrine of St. Ponch, as distant and in disrepair as it undoubtedly still is.*  
  
*Still, his concern for the town of Dreg seemed genuine enough, curiously alluding to the fact that the so called moral conditions had gotten much worse in recent times, on a steady decline for the past ten to fifteen years or so, since the time when his sons were born.*

*Nannigrew wished to remain, pray and ask more questions of him, while Bogfrink and Tip proceeded to the Isle of Shantywood for their supposed spiritual cleansing and meditation. As curious as we were to see the looks on their faces when they realised where they were actually going, it was a good chance to spend time with fellow Mossling Smodri and investigate the parts of town we were more concerned with. As we kept our ears out for rumours and information, Smodri discovered that the Thieves’ Guild, which Tip was most definitely interested in finding, operated out of the Shodgerey's Shack, while we discovered a curious rumour about a Bogle that had taken residence in the smokehouse in the north and was apparently moving things around.*  
  
*We then both spotted a rather elderly and out of place looking woman coming into town, dressed in servant garments that seemed very old and also ill suited for the summer heat. Checking to see if she was alright, it transpired that she appeared not only displaced in space, but in time, thinking she was still in the 900s, when in fact it was actually 1089! It transpired that she had been serving the house of Harrowmoor and performing an errand on their behalf, coming from Castle Brackenwold, only meaning to take a few months at most. She named Lady Persimone rather than Lady Theatrice as the head of House Harrowmoor, and did seem to recognise the name of Tolmen, though obviously Robyn was unfamiliar to her as well.*

*The most she could recall about her curious journey was that she had to hide from a group of individuals on horses in an endless forest of pines and firs, the group all possessing sallow skin and black lips and one wearing a crown of iron thorns. After some reassurances that we would help her return to Prigwort, she expressed curiosity over the fate of ‘Baby Emelda and Baby Eldrin’ and how they might be grown up now! …which is perhaps wishful thinking on her part, but maybe they are not human and can live as long as we or others.*

> *…Vessel Nannigrew praying in meantime. …questioning Vessel Hogbeard about Dreg and decline. …could not put a finger to the exact cause. …stated that vessels were consuming too much fish. …expressed vulnerability in teenage boys to smoked fish. …mentioned group of vessels named “Boghouse Boys”. …farmers, aim to remove the mayor through uprising. …believe that mayor, Vessel Monocleese, is poisoning spouse for sacrifice to a witch. …spouse vessel very ill. …Vessel Nannigrew wants to meet to heal Monocleese spouse vessel…*

*We reconnected with Nannigrew after an hour, though Tip and Bogfrink were nowhere to be found, presumably their foray onto Shantywood Isle was going to keep them occupied for some time. We were all reasonably certain that there was only so much trouble they could get into and only so much of our accumulated funds that they could spend.*

*Deciding to investigate the rumour of the Bogle in the smokehouse, we first proceeded to the cellars, where Smodri used his wood kenning to try and find out the name of the Bogle. No names jumped out as apparent, until upon touching the door to the smokehouse, a rather unusual one appeared: Crewithyant. It sounded bestial, unnatural and almost infectious when translated into Mulch, which set both Smodri and ourself on edge. Further evidence that this was no Boggle seemed to surface as the racks of fish were not tidied, but rather thrown about as if in a struggle or search, or as if some great beast had gone charging through them. There was a quality to the smoke too that seemed very acrid and noxious. The name kept coming up too, proceeding up the stairs.*

*When we reached the second floor, it was clear there was something off, something unnatural, the smoke and fog billowingly thick and dark. As we reached out to sense if there was a malign magical presence on one of the racks-*  
  
> *…CONSUMPTION! …infection! …pathogen! …choke it out!!! BURN IT!!!*

*-we were apparently attacked by a malignant presence, causing our cap to leak rather heavily and a voice to call out that they had not feasted on mushroom for so long. We did point out that either we or Smodri could have provided many had they merely asked, though we suspect at this point that it was meant to be more of a threat or intimidation or boast rather than a genuine benign desire to consume more fungi.*

*Notably, as Smodri tried to communicate with the entity, to confirm if it was different or just some form of corrupted Bogle, a large, dark spiralling horn tried to spear him, bellowing out “Bow to the Nag Lord!” This got the attention of Nannigrew, who rather fortunately was pulling us away from the situation, attempting to parlay by saying that we served the Nag Lord already. Curiously, the creature confirmed this somewhat, saying that it sensed the Nag Lord’s presence deep in Nannigrew and that she should give way to it.*  
  
*Unsure if we should attempt to stop the being, or if we even could given its smoky, incorporeal form and apparent dark power, we all fled, with Nannigrew attempting to seek out Brother Hogbeard to warn him, only to find he was not present at the church at that hour. She did heal our injury, though a worryingly dark pustule remained.*

*Those fears were realised the following morning when we found ourselves much more injured despite resting rather fitfully, apparently in the throes of some form of disease or pestilence.*

*In light of this, we all came together to discuss what we’d learned, including Tip and Bogfrink. We suspected that perhaps that the Nag Lord, or this creature, which Smodri learned from their arts was an “Atacorn” and Spawn of the Nag Lord, was responsible for the decline of Dreg, somehow corrupting or poisoning the town’s fish supply in the smokehouse…which would make Hogbeard’s comment about smoked fish being bad for teenage boys rather curiously apropos? It also perhaps may explain why the mayor’s wife is ill, afflicted with a condition similar to the one we now are presently. There were also some curious revelations and connections around hags and those that summon Atacorn being quite close to the names we saw at the crossroads of “Harridwan House” and “Harridan” and so on.*

*Regardless of our suspicions, it was decided that we remain in Dreg for the time being to confirm some things…unless, of course, our condition worsens…*
