# Chronicler’s notes
Chronicler: Tip

- You congregate early in the morning in the **Hornstoat’s Rest** tavern in **Lankshorn**
- Bogfrink is still swaying slightly after dancing the night away to the beautiful tones of a regular performer in the tavern by **Emelda**; a local woman known for her moving songs about love.
- Nannigrew has been contacted by the parents of Arda Vague (human boy / teenager), who said that he has gone missing. Lately he has travelled out of town along **the Ditchway** to listen to beautiful music he heard near **Droomen Knoll**.
- His parents suspect he has been kidnapped by fairies. They offer 200gp for his safe return, and provide Nannigrew with two cold-iron swords for use against them. As everyone in the party is fey or demi-fey except for Nannigrew, this gift isn’t as useful as it seems.
- The party head north east along the ditchway (spending 2 Travel Points), stopping only to snack on delicious (?!) **nightworm** spawn. Tip discovers they have a pickled taste and pair well with cheese.
- The party advance up Droomen Knoll, passing through fields of blue mallow flowers and silver birches. As they arrive, they find the keep in a decrepit state: sweet-smelling purple vines have reclaimed much of the battlements and one of the two towers has crumbled. Touching the stone, Moggle finds it ice cold.
- Bogfrink hears music and races up the hill, Tip on his tail. Nannigrew prevents Bogfrink from getting closer as they survey 4 rather drunk and/or tired **crookhorns** (breggle who have been corrupted by the Nag-Lord’s influence) mockingly playing drums and trumpets over the sound of haunting pipe music.
- Deciding discretion is the better part of valour, the party head for the pipe music coming from a hole in the rubble. Descending, they find 5 bat-like creatures with oily black feathers and long proboscis hanging from the ceiling.
- On the floor they spy a cloak-covered corpse of a **Drune cottager**. In looting the body (finding a scroll of decipher amongst other treasures), Tip touches an orange crystal that gives them a vision:
	- *A stag’s skeleton twitches on a stone altar, covered in green slime. It is encircled by seven trilithons. A voice intones: `ILL OMENS AT THE WHYTHING STONES. WINTER COMETH.`*
- Needing to get some light in the dungeon, the group bundle through two double doors away from the strange bats. Bogfrink brings some Firelight into the room, revealing six murals of frost elf nobles and courtiers.
	1. Prince Seven-Past-Noon - a bitter man positioned at the head of the table
	2. Lady Silver-So-Mirrored - harp lady 
	3. Sir Dawn-Never-Blooms - icicle beard
	4. Lord Slumber-Bone - portly man
	5. Whisper-of-Candlelight - statuesque woman
	6. Famine-Whistles-Whither - flute man, with three red dogs that were later added to the mural 
- Just as Moggle starts to puts together the jeers from the crookhorns (“faster faster skinny whistle”) with the flautist in the mural, the party hears two small voices singing mocking lullabies in the corridor.
- An ambush is quickly set, with Nannigrew throwing a cloak over the unaware redcaps, Moggle ending one in a single blow and Bogfrink smashing his accordion into the other, who is taken for questioning.
- In an attempt to save his life, Jack-o-Boots spills all, including the name of the crookhorn leader (**Grobnott**) and the location of the boy in Grobnott’s room.
- The party quickly check out some jail cells at the end of the corridor, finding a slightly deranged frost elf playing melodies on a rat-bone pipe. Jack-o-Boots is pressed to the door to check for traps, finding only a blue-flame ward on it that is clearly of arcane nature.
- The elf implores the party to set him free, saying he has served at **Mallowheart’s** pleasure and mercy long enough. In return he offers the location of a pellucidium chalice in a secret room.
- Some good use of lore uncovered in the mural room confirms that **Thinwhistle** and Famine-Whistles-Wither are the same person (although he has forgotten his name), as are Mallowheart and Prince Seven-Past-Noon.
- The party resolve to set a trap for Grobnott in the cells. They check out the other one, finding elven bones and graffiti on the wall “Mallowheart be be cursed”, “Hound-baker Famine-Whistle be double-cursed”.
- Rushing down the corridor the party hear faint clucking from the west and stumble on an underground sundial with a frost gnomon. Bogfrink’s light is quickly set for the shadow to be cast at seven-past-noon, and a great cracking of ice is heard from down the hallway.
- Thinwhistle is freed! He makes great haste past the party, but keeps his end of the bargain by revealing the location of the secret door. On his way out, he disturbs Henry the guard cockerel.
- Moggle conjures an imagined chocolate **Nag-Lord** into Thinwhistle’s now-empty cell and hide in the next door one. Grobnott takes it hook, line, and sinker. Tip seals him in by jamming the locking mechanism shut.
- Racing to his room, they find Arda unconscious but alive, under the effects of a drugging orange smoke. The room is tipped upside down, and Jack-o-Boots is used as a human shield for the second of two times by the party. After taking a poison dart to the chest, he is given a quick conversion to the **One True God** by Nannigrew.
- Tip spots a note written to Grobnott instructing him to place spies within Lankshorn’s constabulary, and is advised to start by contacting one **Rodger Burl**.
- The party loots the room and Moggle leads them, reluctantly, into a snowy hidden room with a frozen pool.
- After the ice is dispelled with mortal blood, Jack-o-Boots is made to swim to retrieve the chalice. As he brings it to the surface, his last hit point is taken from him and he is turned into an icy statue holding aloft a glittering **pellucidium** chalice that swirls with an every-changing potion of **Liquid Time**.
- The party drag the boy up past the bats and make haste to town, delivering him into the arms of grateful parents, in exchange for 200gp.
