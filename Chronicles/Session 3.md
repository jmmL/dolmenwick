# Chronicler’s notes
Chronicler: Ilick

*Three of us began our adventure in the Clashed Antler tavern in southern Prigwort: Joremy Swinney, Bredhr Underbark, and ourselves, Illick Inkcap. We noticed the night before that Bredhr had been very active in trying to drink and ingratiate themselves with a group of human teens, assumedly to try and convert them into the teachings of the Pluritine Church. This was met with hostility and suspicion, as they seemed to regard Bredhr’s Breggle appearance with a rather human-centric distaste, questioning why they were even in Prigwort.*

*We were perhaps fortunate that the group of youths more than likely mistook us for some sort of plant in the corner of the room, owing to our stillness and appearance. We know that Bredhr seemed to express surprise when we responded to her question in the morning. Joremy appeared focused on consuming sustenance for the most part, which we find understandable, given his build and the need for protein he doubtless would require.*

*Regardless, it was soon agreed that we would pursue a notice to attend the Sea of Stars Jeweller in the much more affluent, southern area of Prigwort by someone known as Hague Jerricorn, who was requesting aid. Along the way we passed through the main thoroughfare, where it was Market Day, the focus being food and produce primarily, with festive music aplenty. We were drawn to a fellow Mossling seller by the name of Blobble who provided us with three varietals of edible fungi at a very reasonable price, Bredhr obtained a ten foot pole, and Joremy appeared to be searching for something, though he seemed to give up rather quickly, as if coming to some sort of realisation.*

*The only other place of note on our way through the town was a curious establishment with a purple lacquered door with a sign that read, “Brandybiles, by appointment only. Enquiries to be made at the Wrinkled Meddler.” There was also a warning to would be thieves and rogues that the establishment was under the protection of some sort of shadowy faction. As for what the place actually sold or provided, the only clue was a single dusty, tailored jacket in a display window…the rest of the place seemed quite dark and uninhabited.*

*Arriving at the Sea of Stars Jewellers, we all noted that there seemed to be a heavy guard presence standing outside, worried that perhaps someone had already beaten us to the punch or perhaps we as slightly less well to do looking types, would be turned away immediately. Fortunately, it seemed they were merely there for security purposes and we were ushered inside to meet the proprietor, Hague. She possessed long red hair and a fair countenance, seemingly preoccupied with her business first and foremost.* 

*It transpired that what she needed was someone to go down to the cellar of the establishment where her grandfather, Draxus, supposedly conducted his magical studies before he retired into magical gemcraft and jewellery. While she could not pay us in gold, she promised anything we found down in the place was ours, regardless of how valuable it may be, not holding any sentimental attachment to anything. When questioned as to why she couldn’t just sort through things herself or get a more mundane kind of cleaning or organisation service, she mentioned odd noises and a ‘rather obvious reason’ that we would see when we went down. There also had been one before us a few days prior, a single Mossling by the name of Orrick, who had screamed rather loudly and had since not returned. Thus Bredhr and Joremy came to the agreement that no other adventurers should be sent down until she heard a scream from us, not wanting to have to split any loot any further than necessary. We stayed silent, material possessions have rarely concerned us, the opportunity to see another, much more learned magician’s abode was already tantalising enough.*

*Bredhr in particular was concerned we might need more information before heading down, especially with how cagey Hague seemed to be about the nature of the cellar and her grandfather, and we suggested perhaps consulting a powerful, yet living, magician within Prigwort by the name of Mosslemyre Drouge, though we pointed out given his level of power and importance we may struggle even being able to approach him. This, coupled with the fact Hague seemed entirely fine hiring anyone that walked in, resulted us seizing the window of opportunity and heading down as soon as possible.*

*Perhaps if we had prepared a bit more, things might have turned out differently?*

*Musings aside, it did indeed become apparent why Hague was reluctant to head into her grandfather’s chambers, as the door that lead to them was large, made of some sort of iron or other metal, and was carved with the relief of a huge spider like creature wearing a metal collar and several other jewelled accessories. There was also the matter of Orrick not having come back.*

*Proceeding inwards, we discovered a rather curious set of well carved, pristine ebony and ivory marble statues of what we assumed were meant to depict Draxus himself, which then appeared to be some sort of reminder as to a series of chessboard traps along the floor. Joremy in particular marvelled at the stonework and the sheer effort, craftsmanship and amount of the precious stone was used to build and ornament this place. It was in carefully examining all of these that Bredhr seemed to question us in particular, with it becoming apparent that she had not fully realised we are more than one being, one mind. Our explanations were succinct and as clear as we could make them, and we’re certain we put her mind at ease.*

*In navigating and testing the chessboard traps, the ten foot pole became somewhat more like a seven foot pole from an errant scythe swing…however, our understanding of these traps proved valuable when we discovered a number of giant rats in a dilapidated bedsit, luring them into getting sliced up themselves. Searching the bedsit revealed a small spell book which appeared to be the personal book of one known as “Morlan” with two spells, Light and Vapours of Dream…the latter of which we of course already knew and had prepared.*

*Throughout our explanation, especially in the main corridor, we would now and again hear faint tuneful whistling, which to our ears felt almost like some form of arcane charm magic, so it was agreed we would block our hearing once closer to the source. We also kept seeing a kind of illusion or apparition of a human female thief or rogue running into a room, furniture moving, sobbing and then a loud scream.*

*But beyond the bedsit we discovered a somewhat grim room full of caged skeletons. The skeletons appeared mostly creature-like, a two headed serpent, and overlarge avian…perhaps Draxus was conducting some form of chimerical like research or biological grafting study. What was most notable, however, was the giggling, untethered tones of a man who appeared to be trapped within a mirror in another cage, claiming he was Moreland and was the apprentice of Draxus, having been punished and apparently trapped there for over thirty years or so. This meant we got precious little information of coherent use, but he did seem to find the spider and the whistling music significant for some reason, stating that Draxus did have his ‘pets’.*

*Proceeding further, we were met with an even more curious sight, several well groomed rats all attending another sleek furred rat sitting upon a makeshift throne, who invited us in with a regal greeting, asking for us to attend to her and pay tribute, naming herself Queen Skix. As we provided a delicious fungal tribute we had procured from the market, we got a good look at golden band around her forepaw of clear arcane nature, which seemed to be the source of her awakened intelligence and capacity for speech. Bredhr, seeing a very fresh and receptive face, instantly began to ingratiate Queen Skix with the idea of the Pluritine Church, which seemed to delight her with our group, to the point where she even granted us some of her courtiers to help us hunt some centipedes we had glimpsed in the nearby library for her consuming pleasure. She also warned us of “killer bubbles”. Her kingdom, such as it was, seemed not to extend much further past the room we were in.*

*We approached the library from the unexplored room next to it, finding a kind of lab coated in a number of very fungal looking powders and a couple of corpses of other rats seeming to have died to some sort of poisoning, if the froth around their mouths was anything to go by. Unfortunately we did not think to immediately warn our companions of the dangers of even treading in such a spore infested place, and poor Bredhr immediately caused a cloud of particularly virulent and suffocating spores to blossom out from a purple coat hanging nearby that she wished to search.*

*Both Joremy and ourselves worked tirelessly to try and save her, realising that outside of any magic ability, really the only way to rectify the problem would be to burn out the spores filling her lungs. We attempted what we personally feel is a rather brilliant and unique treatment especially for having come up with it in such dire and time constrained circumstances, using our Thread Whistling to unravel a page from a book Bredhr was carrying and using it like a wick down into her lungs, setting it ablaze. Unfortunately while it appeared to be successful in clearing the spores, it did also seem to burn out her already weakened insides, and she did not wake or recover.*

*Our rat companions were impatient to keep hunting, however, and so we proceeded to confront the centipedes, setting up a trap with the cloak in the lab which managed to lay low two of them much like Bredhr, with the rats swiftly taking out the rest. The library, unfortunately, seemed to be rotted and eaten past the point of usefulness, though it did seem like there was something we were missing, especially as one of our rat companions kept darting towards the wall and disappearing.*

*Continuing around the main corridor, we discovered what was clearly the “killer bubbles,” a large body of liquid spanning the floor where we could see a wooden Mossling axe and a shattered lantern…clearly we had discovered what became of poor Orrick. Testing with the remains of the lantern, it seemed the liquid really only turned to aggressive tendrils pulling a victim into its fifteen foot or so depths if the surface was disturbed in any way. Joremy and ourselves did not have any clear means of traversing this distance safely, but fortunately our rat companion indicated there was possibly another path by running through some hidden crack in a wall and appearing on the other side of the water.*

*Searching the lab and the library as thoroughly as we could, we suddenly beheld that same vision of the female rogue as she appeared, pulled the air in a space near the shelves, went through the wall, sobbed and then screamed. Inspection of the spot revealed a fulcrum one could pull, but since we could hear the faint whistling from before we decided to stopper our ears up with the remainder of the fungi we had purchased so as not to be charmed.*

*On the other side of the wall we beheld, in the chitin, the giant spider depicted on the door! They were in a large room full of pitfalls and silver statues of a woman playing a lyre. It seemed to regard us with slight hostility, and the amulet with Draxus’ initial on it we discovered had no affect when Joremy flashed it, at our assistance. This perhaps is why it went for Joremy first though, and while Joremy put up a brave struggle, and we attempted to put it to sleep beforehand, he was bitten rather horribly by its poisonous chelicerae. We managed to knock it out with our magic, but there was unfortunately nothing we could do for Joremy, so we simply took his spear and slew it, gathering what we could from its corpse.*

*Joremy must have let out a scream when he was bitten, because shortly after we had collected the spider’s choker, an elven woman of a rather opportunistic disposition appeared, identifying herself as Shadow-of-Sorrows-Lament. She seemed suitably impressed in our ability to slay such a creature, which was for the best, as we weren’t likely to be able to defend ourselves if she decided we were in the way of her cut of the loot. Another adventurer joined her, a human cleric by the name of Bertha, who looked a bit more determined to honour all the fallen within this dungeon.*  
  
*Appraising them both of the situation, we banded together to clear out the rest of the space, first discovering a rather ominous looking room with a casket contained in a circle of purple glowing salt and chained shut which we elected not to disturb for now. The next room appeared to be a tapestried room full of Astrological symbology, where we discovered a number of items, one of which at least was very magical, a black and white tome, the gloves we suspect also being so. Then we came across a furnace room with the motifs of Ifrits where there was a large amount of gem and forge based materials, which were eagerly snapped up by the others. Then, finally, we discovered a room with the motifs of jewels being infused with lunar and solar energy, and creatures known as Xorns, where two large sentinels stood guard, ready to repel us, asking for the seal of approval…so Draxus’ medallion was held up and we were able to procure everything within, including a curious Tome of Looping Enchantments.*

*Finally we freed Morlan from his mirror prison, failing to find any other instructions, whereupon he seemed to come more to his senses and, on being questioned, revealed to us that the room with the casket was likely an elaborate trap for him meant to teach him a lesson, which he had now learned. Shadow-of-Sorrows-Lament, taking a bit of a liking to the idea of the rat Queen Skix, decided rather than eliminate her, to warn her that others might be coming to usurp her and to prepare as we left with everything. Including a ring that seemed to be personally addressed to Hague by Draxus…but of course, she had said we could have anything we could find.*

*We do feel deep regret not being able to save our companions. We may not join in any other groups until we’re certain we possess some form of healing within the party…*
