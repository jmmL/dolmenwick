“**Explore** the wild places of the Wood, travelling through bramble-choked dells, fungal forests, and accursed bogland. **Unearth** treasure hoards in forgotten fairy ruins, haunted manors, dripping caverns, crystal grottoes, unhallowed barrow mounds, and abandoned delvings.

**Confront** fell beasts, roving fungal monstrosities, tricksome fairies, and even **defend** all that is good from encroaching forces of the wicked, half-unicorn Nag-Lord who lurks in the corrupted northern woods.

**Forage** for weird fungi and herbs; **chart**leylines and fairy roads; seek the counsel of witches, the mysterious Drune, or abandoned saintly shrines of the Pluritine Church.

**Meddle** in the affairs of nobility, allying with a noble house in its intrigues and power plays in the courts of High-Hankle and Castle Brackenwold.

And, at the end of a long day, **return to the homely hearth** to share tales of peril with quaint locals over a mug of ale and a well-stoked pipe.”

****

During session 0, the following themes were chosen as being particularly interesting to the group:
- Understanding the **Drune** and the **witches**, and what new and old magic they pursue
- Investigating the nature of the **Cold Prince** and his banishment
- Opposing further incursion by the **Nag-Lord**’s forces
