The core rules of Dolmenwood are available online here: https://www.dolmenwood.necroticgnome.com/rules/doku.php?id=start

As mentioned in [[House and optional rules]], we are using most of the Optional rules and a few House rules.

# The four types of rolls

## Ability checks - d6
- Represent innate competence
- Always have a **target of 4**. Get 4 or higher and you succeed
- Are modified by your relevant **ability modifier** (eg STR +2)
- Are something your character does **actively** (eg to move a heavy statue)
- May be granted an **additional modifier** by the referee between -**2 and +2**, depending on the situation
- A roll of a 1 always fails, and a 6 always succeeds
## Skill checks - d6
- Represent learned skills
- Everyone can use **Listen, Search, and Survival** skills
- Certain classes (e.g., Thief, Bard) have **additional skills** (such as Decipher), and they improve at these as they level
- Target starts at **6**, but is reduced for some classes and kindreds
- The need for a Skill Check can be **bypassed by cleverly describing** what your character does (e.g., a Thief could make a Search check for a trap, or they could describe tapping the floor, pouring water over it etc to reveal a pit trap. See Narrative Interaction on DPB 150 for examples and elaboration)
- Are something your character does **actively** (eg to Search for traps)
- May be granted an **additional modifier** by the referee between -**2 and +2**, depending on the situation
- A roll of a 1 always fails, and a 6 always succeeds
## Attack rolls - d20
- Meet or beat the monster’s AC to do damage
- Modified by STR for melee and DEX for missile
- Are something your character does **actively**
- May be granted an **additional modifier** by the referee between -**4 and +4**, depending on the situation
- A roll of a 1 always fails, and a 20 always succeeds
- There are no critical fumbles on Nat 1
- A Nat 20 means you can **maximise the damage dice and add 1**
## Saving throws - d20
- **Meet or beat the relevant save target** on your character sheet, which are:
	- Doom
	- Ray
	- Hold
	- Blast
	- Spell
- If the effect is magical, you can **add your magic resistance modifier** to the die roll (which is equal to your WIS modifier and any other bonuses such as Fairy resistance)
- May be granted an **additional modifier** by the referee between -**4 and +4**, depending on the situation
- A roll of a 1 always fails, and a 20 always succeeds
# Time
- I’ll keep track of time for you, including when things expire like spells
- In a dungeon, time is tracked in 10 minute Turns
- In combat, time passes in 10 second Rounds
- For overland travel, time is tracked in days
# Movement and exploration
## In a dungeon
- Speed per Round is given in feet (e.g, 40) based on your character’s encumbrance, and we are using slot-based encumbrance
- When fleeing combat or running, you move at 3x this speed per Round (provided you are not in melee)
	- Or 10x when fleeing through a mapped dungeon outside of combat
- A party travelling together moves at the speed of the slowest character
- You must take 1 Turn every 6 to rest, or suffer escalating exhaustion penalties
## Overland
- Dolmenwood uses a simple Travel Points (TP) system
- The party has TP equal to (speed of the slowest character) / 5
	- For example, a party with 30 speed has 6 TP
- It costs between 2 and 4 TP to enter a hex
- Fully searching a hex costs the same as entering it, and reveals all hidden areas
	- It also earns you XP! See [[House and optional rules#Feats of Exploration|Feats of Exploration]].
- It costs 2 TP to move 6 miles along a paved road or cleared track
	- If you’re moving along a road or track, you don’t pay any additional costs to enter hexes, but you also only see things near the road
- Getting a good night’s sleep every night and fully resting for one day per week are essential to avoid exhaustion penalties
### Getting lost
- Unless you’re on a road or rowing down a river, there’s always a chance of getting lost in Dolmenwood
- I roll once per day to see whether the party becomes lost
- Chances range from 1-in-6 to 4-in-6, depending on the terrain and the weather
- Hunters (or hired Guides) can help you avoid this fate
# Rests
- An overnight rest restores 1 HP
- A full day resting restores d3 HP
- There are procedures for camping, finding food and firewood etc which give you bonuses to rests. We’ll cover it at the table!
# Combat
- Combat takes place in a phased order
- You can do one action and move
- You can do minor things like shouting a few words or dropping something for free
## Phases
1. **Declarations**
	- Combatants declare whether they are casting a spell/rune or retreating from melee
	- (Additionally the special moves “charge” or “parry” have to be declared)
	- You don’t have to follow through on spellcasting or retreat during the relevant phases, but you cannot decide to do them later
2. **Initiative**
	- Each side rolls a d6, re-rolling ties
	- Within a phase, you can **decide the order** characters act
3. Winning side goes
	1. **Movement**
		- **Those casting spells or runes cannot move**
		- If you are in melee (base-to-base and have attacked or been attacked), you can move half your speed (unless you flee your full speed)
	2. **Missile**
		- Roll attacks using DEX
		- At short range you get **+1** to hit, long range **-1**
		- Shooting into melee or cover can stack penalties up to -4 (similar to disadvantage)
	3. **Magic**
		- If you are hit or fail a save before this phase, you **lose the spell**
		- You must have line of site and cannot move
		- You can also activate magic items and turn undead (no prior declarations needed)
	4. **Melee**
		- Roll attacks using STR
		- Helpless foes (e.g., paralysed) are **hit automatically**
		- Switching your weapon (unless you drop it) - no attack this round
		- See special moves on DPB 168 (Brace, Charge, Two-Weapon Fighting, Push etc)
4. Losing side goes
	- Repeats above phases
5. **Morale**
	- Referee checks morale for monsters, NPCs, and any party retainers
	- Then a **new round** starts from Declarations!
