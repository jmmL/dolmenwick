# Optional rules in effect
- We’re using **all optional rules by default**
- I’ve tried to collect together the most important ones here as a reminder; it’s not an exhaustive list. Check the Dolmenwood Player’s Book (DPB) or with me!
## Skill progression
- Any class with special skills (eg Thief, Bard) can use their customised skill-point based progression instead
## Deaths Door
- When a character goes to 0 HP, they have a 2-in-6 chance of dying per round until magically healed with at least one hitpoint.
- The character permanently loses 1 point from a random ability score on revival.
## Additional combat rules (DPB 168)
- *Attacking with two weapons* - PCs with Dex or Str as prime abilities may choose to wield two one-handed melee weapons, gaining the ability to make an extra attack each Round.
	- Primary weapon: Attack Rolls with the primary weapon suffer a -2 penalty.
	- Secondary weapon: Must be small (e.g. a dagger or hand axe). PC may make one extra attack per Round, at a -4 penalty to Attack Rolls.
	- Breggles can use their horns as a Primary weapon
- *Charging into melee* - Once per combat, a combatant (PC or monster) may charge into melee and attack, as follows.
	- Declaration: Charging must be declared before initiative is rolled.
	- Charge: The combatant must move at least 20′ before attacking. There must be a clear run.
	- Effects: The combatant gains a +2 Attack bonus this Round, but their AC is penalised by -1 this Round.
	- Brace weapons: Weapons that may be braced against a charge inflict double damage on the charging combatant (DPB 118)
- *Missile fire into melee* - The constant and unpredictable movement of melee makes firing or throwing missile weapons at melee combatants an uncertain proposition.
	- Attack penalties: The Attack Roll is penalised by -1 for each combatant in melee with the intended target (to a maximum penalty of -4).
	- *No special effect for getting a Nat 1 - ie no ”critical fumble” where you hit an ally instead*
- You can also *Parry*, *Push*, use S*plash Damage* etc. See DPB 168-9
## Slot encumbrance
- DPB 149 for full explanation
- Everything takes 0-3 slots
- First 100 coins of any type are “free”
- Tiny items - take no slots unless loads of them (eg herbs, fungi, holy symbols, small bells and whistles)
- Bundled items - most consumables can be bundled (eg 3 torches in a slot, 20 arrows, 12 iron spikes etc)
- Clothes you are wearing and containers you’re using don’t take a slot
- Other stuff usually takes 1 slot unless it’s bulky (2 slots) or massive (3 slots eg plate)
## Praying at shrines
Some shrines must be restored before use, which will be apparent on exploring the area
### Holy classes (Cleric, Friar)
- Holy spell-casters who pray for 1 hour at a shrine may receive the blessing of the saint in the form of a specific holy spell.
- One use: The spell bestowed by a saint’s blessing is added to the character’s list of memorised spells. Once cast, it is gone and the saint’s blessing expires.
- One blessing at a time: A character may only have the blessing of a single saint (and therefore only a single additional spell) at any one time. Praying at a shrine dedicated to a different saint causes the spell bestowed by the new saint to replace that bestowed by the previous saint.
- Spells of higher Rank: A spell bestowed by a saint’s blessing may be of higher Rank than the character is usually able to memorise. For example, a Level 2 cleric (normally able to memorise only Rank 1 spells) is able to memorise and cast a single spell of Rank 2 or higher after praying at a shrine. 
- Repeated blessings: Characters may wish to return to a previously visited shrine and pray again. The saint’s blessing is bestowed once more only if the spell is of a Rank that the character can normally memorise and as long as at least one day has passed
### Everyone else
- Requirements: The character must be of Lawful Alignment, Level 3 or higher, and have performed worthy deeds in the service of the Church.
- Once ever: The character can only receive the blessing of each saint once ever
## Optional rules not in effect
- *Training*, DPB 25 (except limited training for Magicians see [[House and optional rules#Learning new spells (DPB 78 alterations)|House Rules]])

# House rules
*Adapted from 3D6 DTL Dolmenwood House Rules.*
## Healthy characters
- All characters start with HP equal whichever is higher of: 1) The roll of their hit dice plus CON modifier *or* 2) the average of their hit dice roll (ie 3 for d4, 4 for d6, 5 for d8)
	- So roll for starting HP; if you get more than the average you can keep it, otherwise take the average
	- You still **roll as normal on level up** (hit dice + CON modifier), gaining a minimum of 1 HP each time
## Critical hits
- On a roll of a natural 20, take the maximum damage you could inflict and add 1 (eg a d8 longsword does 9 damage, plus any relevant modifiers)
- This applies to monsters as well as characters
## Thief backstab
- A successful backstab does 12 damage plus STR (upgraded from 3d4 plus STR)
- This is to reward what is often difficult to set up (target must be unaware of Thief’s presence)
## Shields shall be sundered
- On being hit, a character wearing a shield can elect to instead have their shield be shattered and sundered, rather than taking damage
- If it is a magical shield, this can be instead used a number of times equal to one plus its magic bonus (eg a shield+2 would be sundered on the third use of the ability)
- This effect can’t be used if you are paralysed or otherwise incapable of using the shield to block a blow (eg you are in full retreat, you were surprised)
## Bards’ Decipher skill
- The Deciphers skill for Bards shall also apply to conversations directly overheard and witnessed with eyes and ears; that is, with a successful check a general gist may be understood. This is to reflect the Bards’ skills as oral storytellers.
## Ritual spellcasting
- Magicians can spend 1 Turn per spell rank to cast a rank 1 or 2 spell from their spellbook (without having had to memorise that spell for a day)
## Higher-rank scrolls
- Scrolls of higher ranks can be cast by the appropriate class, consuming them
- They cannot be copied into spellbooks until the caster can memorise spells of that level
- This is to encourage their use as powerful one-time boosts
## Learning new spells (DPB 78 alterations)
- Whenever a magician gets access to spells of a new rank, they must spend a number of days equal to that rank studying with a **mentor** before they are able to memorise a new spell of that rank
	- The mentor must be at least 2 levels higher than the character
	- No gold is expected, but the mentor may expect a favour to be returned, or for you to have already helped them
	- The process is successful providing it is not interrupted
- Spells can be transcribed into an spellbook from scrolls or other spellbooks. 
	- You must be able to read the language of the scroll or spellbook and it must be on the Arcane spell list.
	- It takes 1 day per spell rank.
	- Scrolls are consumed by copying.
	- The process is successful providing it is not interrupted
	- Occasionally, mentors may teach students spells from their spellbooks
## Magical research (DPB 78 alterations)
- Any arcane spell can be researched, in addition to spells granted through levelling up, providing the magician can memorise spells of that rank
	- It takes 1000g per spell rank
	- It takes 2 weeks per spell rank, reduced to 1 with the help of a capable mentor
- Magicians can *invent* new spells. Work with the referee to determine the effects appropriate to the rank
- Magical research has a base 1-in-6 chance of failure (consuming the time and materials)
- Holy and Fairy magic
	- Holy spell casters may, at high levels, be able to invent new spells through great deeds in service of the Church
	- Fairy nobles may occasionally teach powerful runes, although they guard these secrets closely
## Magic item creation
*Taken from Old School Essentials: Advanced Players Tome, p127*
- Creating Magic Items
	- Conversation between player and referee
	- Each class may only make items of their type of magic
	- Often requires rare components or ingredients, and large quantities of gold
	- If the item replicates a spell, it takes 500g and 1 week per spell rank of item duplicated, per use
	- Some items don’t reflect spells precisely (eg +1 weapons or armour). Generally these cost 10k-100k gold and take from 1 month to 1 year to complete
	- There is a base 1-in-6 chance of failure
- Some NPCs around the world can make these to order
	- This (and looting dungeons / getting rewards!) is generally easier than making them yourselves
# Ways to gain XP
- XP is only granted to characters being played in a session
- New characters start with half the XP of the player’s old one, but only if their body was brought back to a safe location and respectfully sent off
## Treasure
- Returning treasure (gold, jewellery, artwork etc) to a safe place with an economy *immediately* grants the party XP
- XP is then divided evenly across the party (assuming there are no retainers etc)
- This is where most of your XP will come from
- Magic items do not grant XP (as they are their own reward), unless they are sold without being used. If so, they grant XP equal to the gold value. Selling magic items isn’t always straightfoward.
## Monsters
- Defeating, forcing to flee, or otherwise outsmarting or overcoming monsters will grant XP
- The referee will try to give this to you at the end of the session (but be patient if there are mistakes or delays!)
- You only get XP from things that pose a threat (no you can’t kill 1000 chickens to level up, unless they’re really mad at you)
## Feats of Exploration
*Original idea from 3D6 DTL Feats of Exploration (OSE), as partially adapted in Dolmenwood Campaign Book.*

- Exploration is often its own reward, BUT, Dolmenwood has lots of cool things to see and interact with, so this will be a minor source of XP
- Feats of Exploration are awarded equally to each party member (ie they aren’t divided per character)
	- So if there’s just two of you playing when you confirm that the mayor is secretly a werewolf, then you don’t get more than if all five people were there that session
- These are “Deed” level awards (200XP each at level 1, but increasing with party level)
- Travel examples
	- Visiting a settlement for the first time
	- Entering a hex for the first time (excluding solely travel on roads or tracks)
	- Finding a lost shrine
	- Finding a nodal stone
	- Discovering other hidden locationd within Dolmenwood
- Social examples
	- Confirming the veracity of a rumour
	- Applying in-world lore in a substantial and useful/flavourful manner
	- Learning a significant secret or interpreting hidden lore
	- Interacting beneficially with an NPC in a high-stakes situation
	- Manipulating a faction to your benefit
	- Solving a significant puzzle or overcoming a substantial trap/hazard
	- Establishing a permanent party safe haven within a dangerous environment (eg not within a settlement)
- Multiple feats can be achieved in a session
- Optionally the referee may award higher grants (Minor or Major) for particular examples
## Carousing
- You get a small amount of XP by spending money on celebrations
- There is a moderate chance of a mishap (WIP, mishap table not player-facing)
### Interim procedure
- Each player can choose a level of carousing per this table. 

| Level      | Min. Settlement | Gold spent per player | Time between carousals |
| ---------- | --------------- | --------------------- | ---------------------- |
| Small      | Village         | 1d6 x 100             | 1 month                |
| Medium     | Small Town      | 1d8 x 150             | 1 week                 |
| Big        | Large Town      | 1d10 x 200            | 3 days                 |
| Really Big | City            | 1d12 x 250            | 1 day                  |

- Get 25% of XP back on gold spent during carousing, 33% during the weekend, or 50% during a major festival
	- *Conversion rate and this whole procedure will probably be changed. Probably to borrow stuff from Shadowdark because that was fun without being too punishing if it goes wrong.*
- Then **Save vs Doom** or lose 10% of your remaining wealth to the Thieves Guild
	-  If, through play, party Thieves have become connected to a settlement’s organised crime elements, they gain a +2 to Save vs Doom, and friends of such-connected thieves get +1.
- If you spend more than you have, then a generous NPC has picked up the tab. They will be in touch with a request…

## Philanthropy
- You get a small amount of XP by spending money on good deeds and works
### Philanthropy procedure
- Once per session while in a settlement, a character may choose to donate to philanthropic causes in order to gain extra experience points.
- They must spend a minimum amount of gold as determined by the size of the settlement.
- They can spend more, but if the amount is more than double, then there is a chance that the populace particularly resents or appreciates the generosity. Roll on the [[House and optional rules#Philanthropic Consequences|Philanthropic consequences]] table
- Gold spent this way is converted to 40% of its value in XP
	- 50% if devoted on a Saint’s Day, or 75% if additionally linked to that Saint’s shrine or main place of worship
	- *Conversion rate subject to change / WIP*

| --------------- | ---------------------- |
| Hamlet          | 100                    |

| Small Town      | 600                    |
| Large Town      | 1000                   |
| City            | 1500                   |
